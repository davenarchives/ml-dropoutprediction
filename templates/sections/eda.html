Exploratory Data Analysis

The visual analysis shows how academic progress, financial standing, and student background separate dropout and non-dropout patterns.

Target Variable Distribution

The dataset is imbalanced: 3,003 students are Non-Dropout while 1,421 are Dropout, so the model needs to learn from fewer dropout examples.

Top 10 Features

Second-semester approved units, semester grades, first-semester progress, and tuition status carry the strongest signal, showing that recent academic performance drives the prediction most.

Distribution by Target

Dropout cases cluster around very low approved units, while non-dropout students concentrate around higher completed units, making approved units a clear risk separator.

Financial Factors by Target

Late tuition payments and lack of scholarship support appear more often among dropout records, suggesting that financial pressure is closely tied to persistence risk.

Demographic Factors

Dropout students trend older at enrollment, and the gender split shows higher dropout share among male students in this dataset, adding useful context alongside academic signals.
